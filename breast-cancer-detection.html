Breast Cancer Detection Neural Network

This program uses the Breast Cancer Wisconsin (Diagnostic) dataset to train and test a neural network that helps identify whether a tumor is malignant or benign. The goal is to support medical decision-making by giving a model a set of diagnostic measurements and having it classify the case as cancerous or noncancerous.

The network takes in 30 input features and passes them through a deep multi-layer architecture before producing a final prediction. For activation, I used ReLU because of its efficiency and because it helps reduce vanishing gradient problems. For optimization, I used Adam with a low learning rate and weight decay to keep the training process stable.

Optimizer used:

torch.optim.Adam(model.parameters(), lr=0.0005, betas=(0.9, 0.999), eps=1e-08, weight_decay=0.0001, amsgrad=False, decoupled_weight_decay=True)

I trained the model for 10,000 epochs, and the final result reached about 99% accuracy after training and testing. I am very happy with that result, and this page shows both the network architecture and the dataset the model was built on.
